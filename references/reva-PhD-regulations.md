## REVA University Regulations Governing the Minimum Standards and Procedure for Award of Doctoral (Ph.D.) Degree, 2025

*(Framed as per the provisions under Section 35 (ii), Section 7 (x) and Section 8 (xvi) \& (xxi) of the REVA University Act, 2012)*

### Preamble

REVA University Regulations Governing the Standards and Procedures for the Degree of Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.) were originally framed on the basis of UGC (Minimum Standards and Procedure for Awards of M.Phil / Ph.D. degree) Regulations, 2009. The UGC subsequently revised these standards and notified the "University Grants Commission (Minimum Standards and Procedure for Awards of M.Phil / Ph.D. degree) Regulations, 2016" on 5th May, 2016, making them mandatory for all Universities / Institutions.

Accordingly, the existing Ph.D. regulations of REVA University have been amended to incorporate the provisions of the 2016 UGC regulations. Further amendments have integrated the Research and Publication Ethics (RPE) course into the Course Work as directed by the UGC vide D.O.No.F.1-1/2018 (Journal/CARE) dated December 2019, and facilitated the transfer of Ph.D. Registration. The present regulation is further amended vide D.O.NO. 1-3/2021(QIP/Ph.D. regulations/post file) dated 14.11.2022.

The present regulation was amended in consultation with the members of the Academic Council in the meeting held on 12/07/2025 and applies to all previous and current batches of Ph.D. Programmes.

## 1\. Title and Commencement

* **1.1** These Regulations shall be called **"REVA UNIVERSITY REGULATIONS GOVERNING THE MINIMUM STANDARDS AND PROCEDURE FOR AWARD OF DOCTORAL (Ph.D.) DEGREE, 2025"**.
* **1.2** These Regulations shall apply to both Full-time and Part-time Ph.D. scholars registered in REVA University to pursue research leading to the award of the Doctoral Degree of REVA University.
* **1.3** These Regulations shall come into force from the date of assent of the Chancellor of REVA University.
* **1.4** The Director-Research and Development Cell shall be responsible for all matters pertaining to Ph.D. Programmes of REVA University.

## 2\. Definitions

In these Regulations, unless the context otherwise requires:

* **2.1 "University"** means REVA University.
* **2.2 "Degree"** means the degree of Doctor of Philosophy (Ph.D.).
* **2.3 "School"** means the School of Studies and Research of REVA University established by the Statutes.
* **2.4 "Department"** means the Department of Studies and Research of REVA University / Constituent Colleges of REVA University.
* **2.5 "Chairperson of the School"** means the Chairperson of the School of Studies and Research of REVA University where a candidate works for a degree.
* **2.6 "Head"** means Head of the School and Research of the University / Head of the Department of Studies of the Constituent Colleges of the University.
* **2.7 "Candidate"** means any person who satisfies the prescribed eligibility condition as stated in Section 3 of the Regulations and who intends to register / has registered for the degree.
* **2.8 "Foreign candidate"** means any person who is a foreign national with a valid foreign Passport satisfying the prescribed eligibility conditions stated in Section 5 and who intends to register / has registered for a degree with a valid research VISA.
* **2.9 "Board of Studies"** means the Post-Graduate Board of Studies in the subject concerned / Joint Boards of Studies in more than one subject / Interdisciplinary Board of Studies as constituted by the University.
* **2.10 "Course work"** means the compulsory preparatory study to be undertaken by a candidate as prescribed by the concerned Board of Studies.
* **2.11 "Guide/supervisor"** means the recognized supervisor for the research work of a candidate satisfying eligibility conditions as specified in Section 4 of the Regulations.
* **2.12 "Co-guide/co-supervisor"** means the recognized supervisor who supervises the Ph.D. work of a candidate jointly with the guide, satisfying eligibility conditions in Section 4 of these Regulations.
* **2.13 "Doctoral Committee / Research Advisory Committee"** means the Committee constituted by the University to oversee the research work of a candidate.
* **2.14 "Board of Examiners"** means the panel of examiners constituted by the University for adjudicating the Ph.D. thesis submitted by a candidate.
* **2.15 "Journal of Repute"** means a journal enlisted in Scopus / Web of Science / UGC Journal list or recognized by the Board of Studies in the concerned discipline / subject / group of subjects.

## 3\. Eligibility Criteria for Admission to the Ph.D. Programme

Subject to the conditions stipulated in these Regulations, the following persons are eligible to seek admission to the Ph.D. Programme:

* **3.1** Candidates for admission to the Ph.D. Programme shall have a Master's degree or a professional degree declared equivalent to the Master's degree by the corresponding statutory regulatory body, with at least **55% marks in aggregate** or its equivalent grade 'B' in the UGC 7-point scale (or an equivalent grade in a point scale wherever a grading system is followed), or an equivalent degree from a foreign educational institution accredited by an approved Assessment and Accreditation Agency in its home country.
* **3.2** Candidates who have cleared the M.Phil. course work with at least 55% marks in aggregate (or its equivalent grade 'B' in the UGC 7-point scale) and successfully completed the M.Phil. Degree shall be eligible to proceed to research work leading to the Ph.D. Degree in the same Institution in an integrated Programme.
* **3.3** A relaxation of **5% of marks (from 55% to 50%)**, or an equivalent relaxation of grade, is allowed for those belonging to SC/ST/OBC (non-creamy layer) / differently-abled and other categories of candidates as per the decision of the UGC from time to time.
* **3.4** The eligibility marks of 55% and the relaxation of 5% to the categories mentioned above are permissible based only on the qualifying marks without including grace mark procedures.
* **3.5** A person whose M.Phil. dissertation has been evaluated and the viva voce is pending may be provisionally admitted to the Ph.D. Programme. This admission is automatically withdrawn if the candidate fails to submit the marks/grade cards within one month after the closing date of admissions or fails to pass the M.Phil. with the required marks/grades.
* **3.6** Candidates possessing a Degree considered equivalent to an M.Phil. Degree from an accredited Foreign Educational Institution shall be eligible for admission.
* **3.7** Experts from industries possessing any Master's degree can be permitted to pursue research in Computer Science and Applications to encourage continuous learning. Such candidates must undergo **two semesters of course work with a total credit of 30** (as per Section 9.7 c).
* **3.8** Candidates from abroad with a relevant degree should submit an equivalence certificate. If an equivalence certificate is not provided but the candidate holds a valid degree from abroad under Section 3.1, an internal committee constituted by the Vice Chancellor can permit them to pursue research after completing a total of **50 credits** (under Section 9.7 d).
* **3.9** Candidates with a **four-year degree Programme with a minimum of 70% aggregate** or equivalent grade in the UGC 7-point scale are eligible. Such candidates must undergo **three semesters of course work with a total credit of 46** (under section 9.7 b).
* **3.10** Candidates eligible under Section 3.1 who wish to pursue research in domains other than the domain in which their degree was awarded must undergo **three semesters of coursework with a total of 50 credits** (under section 9.7 d). However, if the candidate possesses industrial experience of over five years in the domain field and demonstrates sufficient knowledge, the doctoral committee can permit the candidate to pursue research with **one semester of course work**.
* **3.11 Interdisciplinary Research:** Candidates eligible under Section 3.1 who wish to pursue an interdisciplinary research Programme may be granted permission as per NEP 2020 to provide academic flexibility.

  * **3.11.1** Candidates may apply for an interdisciplinary Ph.D. at the time of admission.
  * **3.11.2** The research proposal must clearly articulate the interdisciplinary nature of the study. Applicants from diverse backgrounds must demonstrate sufficient knowledge in the proposed area. The Doctoral Advisory Committee (DAC) will assess feasibility and may recommend additional coursework or suggest appointing a co-guide from another school or an external expert.
* **3.12 Change to Interdisciplinary Research Midway:**

  * **3.12.1** Enrolled scholars may apply to transition to an interdisciplinary research topic by submitting a revised research proposal approved by their primary supervisor and endorsed by the DAC and the Director of the R\&D Cell. Additional coursework must be completed if required.
* **3.13** Interdisciplinary Ph.D. candidates are required to have at least one primary supervisor and one co-supervisor from distinct academic disciplines. An industry expert or scientist may be appointed as an external mentor for industry-relevant topics.
* **3.14 Interdisciplinary Course Work:** Scholars must complete coursework encompassing subjects from both primary and secondary disciplines. The credit structure should promote integration while maintaining depth.

## 4\. Duration of the Programme

* **4.1** The Ph.D. Programme shall be for a **minimum duration of THREE years** counted from the date mentioned in the provisional letter, including course work.
* **4.2** It can be extended to **six years normally, and up to eight years in special cases** (e.g., medical issues or a pandemic) with the approval of a committee comprising the Vice Chancellor, Director R\&D, and the Doctoral Committee.
* **4.3** Extension beyond 8 years due to unavoidable circumstances may be reviewed and decided on a case-to-case basis by the university authority.
* **4.4** Women candidates and persons with disability (more than 40% disability) may be allowed a **relaxation of two years** in the maximum duration. Additionally, women candidates may be provided Maternity Leave / Child Care Leave once in the entire duration for **up to 240 days**.
* **4.5** If a candidate fails to submit the Thesis even after **TEN years**, their Ph.D. Registration is annulled. Such a candidate must restart the admission process from the beginning if they wish to work again.
* **4.6** For candidates with a four-year Bachelor's degree/B.Sc. Research, candidates pursuing a different domain, or foreign candidates undergoing three semesters of coursework, the **minimum duration is FOUR years and the maximum is TEN years**.

## 5\. Procedure for Admission

* **5.1** Admission to Ph.D. shall be through an **Entrance Test and Interview only**.
* **5.2** The University shall decide the annual intake of Ph.D. scholars based on the availability of Research Supervisors, scholar-teacher ratios, laboratory, library, and other academic facilities.
* **5.3** The University shall invite applications via website notifications and newspaper advertisements. An additional application cycle may be conducted to attract candidates from premier institutions or those supported by external fellowships. Candidates who failed the most recent cycle are ineligible for the special drive.
* **5.4 Entrance Test:**

  * **5.4.1** Each School shall have a School Research Committee comprising the Director of the School as Chairperson and all research guides as members.
  * **5.4.2** The Director R\&D is responsible for conducting the entrance test, evaluation, interviews, and preparing the merit list.
  * **5.4.3 Test Framework:**

    * **a)** For general candidates, the test consists of **50% Research Methodology and 50% Subject Specific** components.
    * **b)** For M.Phil. graduates, the syllabus is entirely **Subject Specific (100 marks, 3 hours duration)**.
    * **c)** Candidates who have qualified UGC-NET (including JRF/SRF) / UGC-CSIR NET (including JRF) / GATE / CEED scores are **exempted from the entrance test** and are directly eligible for the interview.
    * **d)** Foreign Students with a **CGPA of 4.8/5 and above are exempted** from the entrance test.
    * **e)** For candidates from abroad, the test and interview can be conducted online.
    * **f)** Candidates in their final year of a PG / B.Sc. Research Programme are eligible to appear for the test.
  * **5.4.4** Candidates securing a minimum of **50% marks in the entrance examination** shall be declared eligible for the interview/viva voce (with a 5% relaxation for SC/ST/OBC/differently-abled categories).
* **5.5** Eligible candidates will be invited by the School Research Committee to discuss their research interests through a presentation.
* **5.6** The interview/viva voce evaluates candidate competence, institutional suitability, and the research’s contribution to new knowledge.
* **5.7** The interview is assessed out of **50 marks**. Selection is based on a weightage of **70% for the Entrance Test and 30% for the Interview**, subject to guide availability.
* **5.8** The finalized list of selected candidates is approved by the Vice Chancellor and notified with a deadline for admission.
* **5.9** Candidates who pass the test but cannot secure a seat due to lack of vacancy can be selected in the preceding academic year. **Entrance scores are valid for two years**.
* **5.10** Non-selected candidates must re-apply and clear the test afresh in future cycles.

## 6\. Provisional Registration

* **6.1** Selected candidates must apply for Provisional Registration after paying the stipulated tuition fee. For the 2025 batch onwards, the provisional registration date is **October 30th** of that academic year.
* **6.2** The R\&D Cell issues a provisional registration letter specifying the assigned Guide, Co-Guide (if any), and applicable terms.
* **6.3** Candidates must report to the School Director, produce their fee receipt, and commence research.
* **6.4** The University maintains a year-wise list of all registered Ph.D. students on its website.
* **6.5 Provision for Transfer of Ph.D. Registration:** Scholars registered at other universities may seek transfer to REVA University provided:

  * **i.** Coursework has been successfully completed and progress is satisfactory.
  * **ii.** The parent guide issues a No Objection Certificate (NoC) and progress report.
  * **iii.** The parent university issues a Transfer Certificate and coursework completion report.
  * **iv.** A REVA University faculty member agrees to supervise the candidate and continue the existing topic.
  * **v.** The scholar agrees to abide by REVA University regulations and completes research at REVA for a **minimum period of 2 years**.
* **6.6** Documents required for transfer include academic records, parent guide NoC, Transfer Certificate, REVA supervisor acceptance certificate, and a compliance declaration.
* **6.7** If coursework was not completed at the parent university, the scholar must undergo coursework at REVA University.

## 7\. Eligibility Criteria for Recognition as Research Guide / Co-guide

* **7.1 Eligibility Standards:**

  * **a.** **Regular Professors** with at least **FIVE research publications** in refereed journals, and **Regular Associate/Assistant Professors** with a Ph.D. and at least **THREE research publications** in refereed journals. Faculty must have completed one year of service and published **one Q1/Q2/Q3/UGC Care listed journal article** as first or corresponding author *after* the award of their Ph.D.
  * **b.** Relaxations may be granted in disciplines where referred journals are limited, with reasons recorded in writing.
  * **c.** Only full-time regular teachers of REVA University can act as supervisors; external main supervisors are not allowed. Co-supervisors from other schools, government institutions, or external organizations are permitted for interdisciplinary work.
  * **d. Interdisciplinary Co-guides:** Limited to **only one** co-supervisor. An additional industry expert or scientist may be designated as a research mentor to permit co-authorship.
* **7.2 Allocation of Research Supervisors:**

  * **a.** Determined by the concerned school based on specialization matches, current scholar load, and student research interests.
  * **b.** External/cross-school supervisors or co-supervisors can be assigned under special matching requirements.
  * **c. Guide Assignment Caps:**

    * **Professor:** Maximum **8** Ph.D. scholars.
    * **Associate Professor:** Maximum **6** Ph.D. scholars.
    * **Assistant Professor:** Maximum **4** Ph.D. scholars.
    * Each supervisor can guide **two international scholars** over and above these limits.
  * **d.** Faculty with less than 3 years of service remaining before superannuation cannot take new scholars. They may continue guiding existing scholars until superannuation and act as co-supervisors post-retirement. **The absolute maximum age for any supervisor is 70 years**.
  * **e. Relocation \& Transfers:** Relocating women scholars (due to marriage or other reasons) can transfer their research data to another university, provided the work does not belong to an institutionally secured project. If a supervisor transfers to another institution, they may continue as a co-guide upon submitting an NoC. They can remain the main guide if the scholar has already submitted their long synopsis.

## 8\. Doctoral Committee / Research Advisory Committee

* **8.1 Composition:**

  * **a.** The Research Guide and Co-guide (if any).
  * **b.** Director / Head of the School concerned (Chairperson).
  * **c.** Two Senior Faculty Members / Scientists from the School with domain expertise.
  * **d.** One External Member from a reputable institute with good research credentials.
  * **e.** For interdisciplinary topics, at least one expert from another school.

  The committee conducts progress reviews **biannually**. To streamline workflows, core groups within schools can share one external expert for every 3-4 scholars. No more than one review session per day may involve the same external expert.

* **8.2** If the Guide is also the School Director, an additional senior faculty member joins the committee. If a school has fewer than two faculty members, sister schools supply the representatives.
* **8.3 Key Responsibilities:**

  * **a.** Review research proposals and finalize topics.
  * **b.** Guide study design, methodology, and identify required courses.
  * **c.** Periodically review and assist in research progress.
  * **d.** Conduct pre-registration colloquiums.
  * **e.** Submit progress remarks to the R\&D Cell.
  * **f.** Conduct pre-thesis submission colloquiums.
  * **g.** Participate in the open viva-voce.
The same committee remains intact until the scholar submits their thesis.

### 9.8 Continuous Assessment, Earning of Credits and Award of Grades

The assessment happens continuously but is divided into 3 main reporting phases: Internal Assessment I (IA-I), Internal Assessment II (IA-II), and Course Work End Examination (CWEE).

* **9.8.2 Internal Assessment (IA) - I (25 Marks):** Completed by the 10th week, covering the first 50% of the syllabus (Units 1 \& 2; Units 1-3 for RPE).

  * *Seminar:* 5 marks
  * *Assignment:* 5 marks
  * *Review Test:* 15 marks
* **9.8.3 Internal Assessment (IA) - II (25 Marks):** Consolidated during the 20th week, covering the remaining 50% of the syllabus (Units 3 \& 4; Units 4-6 for RPE).

  * *Seminar/Assignment/Test split identical to IA-I.*
  * *Week 21 is designated for revision. Results are announced by the 26th week.*
* **9.8.4 Examination Execution:** Question papers are set by internal school members. The R\&D Cell, in coordination with the Registrar (Evaluation) and appointed School Coordinators, manages operations.

### 9.9 Course Evaluation Matrices

#### 9.9.1 Review of Literature Evaluation Split

* **IA-I (25%):** Periodic Progress and First Progress Report followed by Seminar.
* **IA-II (25%):** Periodic Progress and Second Progress Report followed by Seminar (focuses on problem identification, hypotheses, and methodology).
* **CWEE (50%):** Final dissertation report evaluation (30%) and Viva-Voce examination (20%).

#### 9.9.2 Research Publication Ethics (RPE) Marks Scaling

* **IA-I:** Internal Test-1 conducted for 20 marks, scaled down to **10 Marks**.
* **IA-II:** Internal Test-2 conducted for 20 marks, scaled down to **10 Marks**.
* **CWEE:** Final exam conducted for 50 marks, scaled down to **30 Marks**.

## 11\. Pre-registration Colloquium

* **11.1** Upon clearing the coursework with the required grades, the scholar must develop their full research proposal.
* **11.2** A synopsis (research proposal) must be prepared in consultation with the Guide and submitted (**two copies**) within **one month** for registration.
* **11.3 Synopsis Specifications:** Must include a background study, literature review, justification, defined concepts, objectives, hypotheses, data collection methodology, analytical techniques, limitations, and a structural outline of the expected report.
* **11.4** The School organizes a Doctoral Committee meeting to conduct the Pre-registration Colloquium.
* **11.5** The scholar presents a technical report and work plan before the Doctoral Committee.
* **11.6** The Committee assesses preparedness and can mandate scope, title, or methodological updates. If findings are unsatisfactory, the candidate may be asked to present the colloquium again.
* **11.7** Decision outcomes are routed to the Director of the R\&D Cell within one week.

\---

## 12\. Ph.D. Registration

* **12.1** Within **15 days** of receiving positive recommendations, the scholar must apply for formal Ph.D. Registration, enclosing two physical copies and one soft copy of the finalized synopsis.
* **12.2** Failure to apply within **ONE month** results in forfeiture of registration claims, requiring the candidate to clear the entrance test process again.
* **12.3** The R\&D Cell notifies formal registration approvals within two weeks.
* **12.4** The effective date of registration tracks back to the initial **Provisional Registration date**.

\---

## 13\. Progress of Work

* **13.1** Progress reports endorsed by the guide must be submitted to the R\&D Cell **once every Six months**, accompanied by two intermediate reports. Scholars must present their progress to the Doctoral Committee every six months. Foreign students can present half-yearly updates online but must complete annual reviews offline.
* **13.6** Unsatisfactory tracks prompt written corrective warnings. If unaddressed, the committee may recommend cancellation of the registration.
* **13.7** Failure to submit **two consecutive research progress reports** results in the cancellation of registration.
